Black Holes

RESEARCH

Black holes are regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing‚Äîno particles or even electromagnetic radiation such as light‚Äîcan escape from it. NASA studies these phenomena through various space telescopes.

Types of Black Holes

Stellar Black Holes

Formed by the gravitational collapse of a star, these black holes have masses ranging from about 5 to several tens of solar masses.

Supermassive Black Holes

Found at the center of most galaxies, including our Milky Way. They can have masses millions or billions times that of our sun.

Intermediate Black Holes

Ranging from 100 to 100,000 solar masses, these are less common and still being studied.

Recent Discovery

In 2019, the Event Horizon Telescope captured the first image of a black hole's shadow (M87*).

International Space Station

ORBITAL OUTPOST

The International Space Station (ISS) is a modular space station in low Earth orbit. It serves as a microgravity and space environment research laboratory where scientific research is conducted in astrobiology, astronomy, meteorology, physics, and other fields.

Current Research

Microgravity Effects

Studies on how microgravity affects human physiology, including bone density, muscle atrophy, and cardiovascular health.

Advanced Materials

Production of advanced materials impossible to create on Earth due to gravity interference.

Radiation Studies

Measuring space radiation effects on living organisms to prepare for long-duration space missions.

Earth Observation

Monitoring Earth's climate, natural disasters, and environmental changes from orbit.
